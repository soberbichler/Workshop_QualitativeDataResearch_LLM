Large Language Models for Qualitative Data Analysis

Instructor: Sarah Oberbichler (Leibniz Institute of European History, Mainz)

Abstract:

Recent AI developments have opened new perspectives for qualitative research. While corpus-based discourse or content analysis traditionally treated texts as a "bag of words" and identified linguistic patterns based on frequency distributions, Large Language Models (LLMs) offer a significantly more profound ability to process language, semantics, and context.

Workshop Overview:

This workshop will introduce how LLMs can be integrated into qualitative research by providing insights into their capabilities and limitations, alongside hands-on training in:

  • Prompt engineering for qualitative analysis
  • Model implementation in research workflows
  • Critical bias evaluation techniques
  • Practical analysis of earthquake newspaper reporting and their argumentative units

Key Learning Outcomes:

  • Understanding LLM capabilities beyond traditional text analysis
  • Hands-on experience with semantic and contextual processing
  • Critical evaluation skills for AI-assisted research
